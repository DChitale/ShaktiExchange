Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about P2P energy trading

P2P energy trading allows individuals and businesses to buy and sell electricity directly with each other, bypassing traditional utility companies. Using blockchain technology and smart contracts, participants can trade renewable energy at competitive prices within their local community.

Blockchain creates an immutable, transparent ledger of all energy transactions. Smart contracts automatically execute trades when conditions are met, eliminating intermediaries and reducing fraud risk. Each transaction is cryptographically secured and verified by the network.

Anyone with renewable energy generation (like solar panels), energy storage systems, or even just energy consumption can participate. This includes homeowners, businesses, community organizations, and energy cooperatives.

Pricing is determined by real-time supply and demand within the local energy market. Our algorithm matches buyers and sellers based on their preferences, current grid conditions, and market rates, ensuring fair and competitive pricing for all participants.

You need a smart meter for accurate energy monitoring, and optionally renewable energy generation (solar panels) or storage systems. Our platform integrates with most existing smart home and energy management systems.

P2P energy trading is legal in many jurisdictions and is increasingly supported by regulatory frameworks worldwide. We ensure compliance with all local energy regulations and work closely with utilities and regulatory bodies.

Payments are automatically processed through our secure payment system integrated with smart contracts. You can link bank accounts, credit cards, or use cryptocurrency. All transactions are recorded on the blockchain for transparency.

When your renewable sources aren't producing energy, you can purchase from other community members who have excess generation or storage. The system automatically switches between buying and selling based on your real-time energy needs.
